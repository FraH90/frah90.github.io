The DFT assumes the $N$-point signal is one period of an infinitely periodic sequence.
When the signal frequency is not an integer multiple of $\Delta f = f_s / N$, the assumption is violated
and energy "leaks" from the true frequency bin into neighboring bins. This is **spectral leakage**.

## Why It Happens

Multiplying a signal by a rectangular window (selecting $N$ samples) is equivalent to convolving the
spectrum with a sinc function:

$$W_{\text{rect}}(f) = \frac{\sin(\pi f N / f_s)}{\sin(\pi f / f_s)}$$

The sinc has high sidelobes (−13 dB first sidelobe). Any spectral component leaks into adjacent bins
via these sidelobes.

## Windowing as the Fix

A window function $w[n]$ tapers the signal to zero at the edges before taking the DFT:

| Window | 1st Sidelobe | Main Lobe Width |
|---|---|---|
| Rectangular | −13 dB | $2/N$ |
| Hann | −32 dB | $4/N$ |
| Hamming | −43 dB | $4/N$ |
| Blackman | −58 dB | $6/N$ |

The trade-off: better sidelobe suppression always comes with a wider main lobe (lower frequency resolution).
