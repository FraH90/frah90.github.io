When a transmission line is terminated in a load impedance $Z_L \neq Z_0$, not all the incident power is absorbed by the load.
The remainder is reflected back toward the source, creating a **standing wave** along the line.

## Reflection Coefficient

The voltage reflection coefficient at the load is:

$$\Gamma_L = \frac{Z_L - Z_0}{Z_L + Z_0}$$

$|\Gamma_L|$ ranges from 0 (perfect match) to 1 (total reflection — short or open circuit).

## Voltage Standing Wave Ratio

$$\text{VSWR} = \frac{1 + |\Gamma_L|}{1 - |\Gamma_L|}$$

VSWR = 1 means perfect match. VSWR = ∞ means total reflection.

## Special Cases

- **Matched load** ($Z_L = Z_0$): $\Gamma = 0$, no standing waves, all power delivered.
- **Short circuit** ($Z_L = 0$): $\Gamma = -1$, voltage null at the load.
- **Open circuit** ($Z_L \to \infty$): $\Gamma = +1$, voltage maximum at the load.
