When a transmission line is terminated in a load impedance $Z_L \neq Z_0$, not all the incident
power is absorbed. The remainder reflects back toward the source, creating a **standing wave** pattern.

## Reflection Coefficient

The voltage reflection coefficient at the load:

$$\Gamma_L = \frac{Z_L - Z_0}{Z_L + Z_0}$$

$|\Gamma_L|$ ranges from 0 (perfect match) to 1 (total reflection — short or open circuit).

The voltage along the line is the superposition of incident and reflected waves:

$$V(z) = V^+\!\left(e^{-j\beta z} + \Gamma_L\, e^{+j\beta z}\right)$$

## Voltage Standing Wave Ratio

$$\text{VSWR} = \frac{1 + |\Gamma_L|}{1 - |\Gamma_L|}$$

VSWR = 1 means perfect match. VSWR = ∞ means total reflection.

## Special cases

- **Matched** ($Z_L = Z_0$): $\Gamma = 0$, flat voltage profile, all power delivered.
- **Short circuit** ($Z_L = 0$): $\Gamma = -1$, voltage null at the load, current maximum.
- **Open circuit** ($Z_L \to \infty$): $\Gamma = +1$, voltage maximum at the load, current null.

The distance between adjacent voltage minima is always $\lambda/2$, regardless of load.
A voltage minimum is located $\lambda/4$ from a short-circuit termination — this is the basis of the quarter-wave transformer.
